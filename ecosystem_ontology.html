Ecosystem ontology

Descriptions and Internationalised Resource Identifiers (IRI) for natural ecosystems

AQUATIC - Freshwater

Lake or pond

ENVO:01000252

A water body of more than three meters of width with stagnant freshwater.

River or stream

ENVO:01000253

A water body of more than one meter of width where freshwater flows.

AQUATIC -  Marine

Epeiric / epicontinental sea

ENVO:01000045

A shallow sea that extends over part of a continent. It is usually associated with the marine transgressions of the geologic past and are occasionally semi-cyclic.

Estuarine

ENVO:01000020

Biomes where rivers flow into a sea. Estuaries experience tidal flows and their water is a changing mixture of fresh and salt.

Marginal sea

ENVO:01000046

Comprises parts of an ocean partially enclosed by land such as islands, archipelagos, or peninsulas. Marginal seas have ocean currents caused by ocean winds.

Marine benthic

ENVO:01000024

Encompasses the seafloor and includes areas such as shores, marine coral reefs, and the deep seabed.

Marine pelagic

ENVO:01000023

The marine water column - from the surface to the greatest depths.

Marine salt marsh

ENVO:01000022

Marshes that are transitional intertidals between land and salty or brackish marine water (e.g.: sloughs, bays, estuaries).

Marine upwelling

ENVO:01000858

A marine biome which contains communities adapted to living in an environment determined by an upwelling process.

Mediterranean sea

ENVO:01000047

Mostly enclosed seas that have limited exchange of deep water with outer oceans and the water circulation is dominated by salinity and temperature differences rather than winds.

Ocean

ENVO:01000048

Major, continuous bodies of saline water that are customarily divided into several principal oceans and smaller seas.

TERRESTRIAL - Anthropogenic

Cropland

ENVO:01000245

An environment that has been modified by humans by the preparation of the land, usually for the purposes of growing crops.

Dense settlement

ENVO:01000248

An anthropogenic terrestrial biome that is primarily used for human habitation, recreation, and industry within built structures with little other land use.

Rangeland

ENVO:01000247

An anthropogenic terrestrial biome that is primarily used for the rearing and grazing of livestock.

Village

ENVO:01000246

An anthropogenic terrestrial biome that contains settlements such as villages, towns, and/or small cities. Also described as agricultural settlement with greater than 100 persons per square kilometer.

TERRESTRIAL - Desert

Montane desert

ENVO:01000185

Occurs in regions elevated above sea level and which has community structure determined by elevation-dependent environmental conditions.

Polar desert

ENVO:01000186

Subject to low levels of insolation, annual precipitation less than 250 millimeters, and a mean temperature less than 10°C during its warmest month.

Subtropical desert

ENVO:01000184

Has communities adapted to temperatures above a monthly average of 18°C, high daily temperature ranges of ca 20°C, high insolation (but lower than that of a tropical desert biome), inhibited cloud formation, and very low precipitation.

Temperate desert

ENVO:01000182

Has communities adapted to intermediate insolation, low rainfall, and large temperature variations between winter and summer. Winter temperatures may fall below 0°C.

Tropical desert

ENVO:01000183

Has communities adapted to temperatures above a monthly average of 18°C, high daily temperature ranges of approximately 20°C, very high insolation due to inhibited cloud formation, and very low precipitation.

TERRESTRIAL - Forest

Broadleaf forest

ENVO:01000197

A forest biome that contains densely packed populations or communities of broadleaf trees, strongly limiting light penetration to the forest floor.

Coniferous forest

ENVO:01000196

A forest biome that contains densely packed populations or communities of coniferous trees, strongly limiting light penetration to the forest floor.

Mediterranean forest

ENVO:01000199

A forest ecosystem that includes communities adapted to 1) hot to warm, dry summers, 2) mild to cold, rainy winters, and 3) the influence of large, usually marine, bodies of water.

Mixed forest

ENVO:01000198

A forest ecosystem that contains densely packed populations or communities of broadleaf AND coniferous trees, strongly limiting light penetration to the forest floor.

Tropical forest 

ENVO:01001803

Occurs near the equator and has distinct seasonality: winter is absent, and only two seasons are present (rainy and dry) dominated by trees and other woody vegetation.

Temperate forest

ENVO:01001805

Has well-defined seasons with a distinct winter. Moderate climate and dominated by trees and other woody vegetation.

Boreal forests or taiga

ENVO:01000250

Seasons are divided into short, moist, and moderately warm summers and long, cold, and dry winters. Communities are dominated by trees and other woody vegetation.

TERRESTRIAL - Grassland

Flooded grassland

ENVO:01000195

A flooded grassland ecosystem (which is a biome, in which grasses are the dominant form of vegetation and determine the system's structure and dynamics). 

Montane grassland

ENVO:01000194

Occurs in regions elevated above sea level and which has community structure determined by elevation-dependent environmental conditions.

Subtropical grassland 

ENVO:01000191

A subtropical grassland in which dominant ecological communities have reached their climax successional state.

Temperate grassland

ENVO:01000193

Characterised as having grasses as the dominant vegetation, while trees and large shrubs are absent. Temperatures vary more from summer to winter, and the amount of rainfall is less in temperate grasslands than in savannas.

Tropical grassland 

ENVO:01000192

A grassland ecosystem is characterised by lands dominated by grasses rather than large shrubs or trees which are subject to tropical climatic conditions.

TERRESTRIAL - Mangrove

Mangrove

ENVO:01000181

A terrestrial biome that includes mangrove plants, which are able to withstand high levels of salinity as well as regions of anoxia and frequent tidal inundation. They often occur near tropical and sub-tropical estuaries.

TERRESTRIAL - Schrubland

Montane shrubland

ENVO:01000216

Occurs in regions elevated above sea level and has a community structure determined by elevation-dependent environmental conditions.

Subtropical shrubland

ENVO:01000213

Dominated by shrubs located in humid climate regions of tropical latitudes.

Temperate shrubland

ENVO:01000215

Tend to range between the 30- and 40-degree marks of north and south latitude and often occur in areas with varying or little rainfall. Includes areas such as chaparrals, woodlands, and savannas.

Tidal mangrove shrubland

ENVO:01001369

A shrubland biome which is densely vegetated by dwarf or short mangroves that are generally less than 6 meters in height, is tidally influenced, and is located in tropical or subtropical areas.

Tropical shrubland

ENVO:01000214

Shrubland that is dominated by woody or herbaceous shrubs, located in semi-arid to semi-humid climate regions of tropical latitudes.

Xeric shrubland (or desert)

ENVO:01000218

Evaporation exceeds rainfall, usually less than 250mm (10 inches) per year, dominated by woody or herbaceous shrubs.

TERRESTRIAL - Tundra

Alpine tundra

ENVO:01001505

Exists at high altitudes and where vegetation - dominated by a few species of dwarf shrubs, a few grasses, sedges, lichens, and mosses - is stunted due to low temperatures and high winds. Trees are absent.

Polar Tundra

ENVO:01001625

Subject to polar climatic conditions encircling the north pole and the south pole. The Arctic tundra extending south to the coniferous forests of the taiga.

TERRESTRIAL - Woodland

Savanna

ENVO:01000178

A woodland biome which has seasonal water availability and includes trees spaced sufficiently far apart to allow an unbroken layer of grass, sedge or rush communities to live.

Subtropical woodland

ENVO:01000222

A low-density forest which is subject to subtropical climate patterns.

Temperate woodland

ENVO:01000221

Similar to a forest but with wider spacing between trees and sparse lower strata. Characterised by either deciduous or evergreen, broad-leaved or needle-leaved. Temperate climate ranging from semi-arid to semi-humid.

Tropical woodland

ENVO:01000220

Similar to a forest but with wider spacing between trees and sparse lower strata. Characterised by evergreen shrubs and seasonal graminoids; the climate is warm and moist.

TERRESTRIAL - Wetland

Peatland

ENVO:00000044

Wetland that accumulates acidic peat, a deposit of dead plant material. Usually inundated or saturated by surface or ground water at a frequency sufficient to support vegetation typically adapted for life in saturated soil conditions.

Riparian

ENVO:0000xxxx

Interface between land and a river or stream.

Marsh

ENVO:00000035

Includes communities of grasses, rushes, reeds, typhas, sedges, and other herbaceous plants.

Mudflat 

ENVO:00000192

A wetland that forms when mud is deposited by the tides, rivers, sea, or oceans.

Intertidal zone

ENVO:01001468

Areas of the foreshore and seabed that are exposed to the air at low tide and submerged at high tide.
